Anime basics: Where to begin

The first thing to understand when getting into anime is how genres work within Japanese animation. Much like in Western media, genres in anime are largely about marketing and target audiences rather than strict storytelling rules. Anime is generally divided into four main demographic categories. Shōnen is aimed at young boys and often features action, adventure, and themes of friendship and growth. Shōjo targets young girls and typically focuses on relationships, emotions, and personal development. Seinen is created for adult men and tends to explore more complex, mature, or darker themes, while Josei is aimed at adult women and often presents realistic portrayals of romance, work, and everyday life.

Its important to note that these categories dont limit who can enjoy a series—they simply describe who the show was originally made for. Anyone can enjoy anime from any category, and many popular shows blend elements from multiple demographics.
